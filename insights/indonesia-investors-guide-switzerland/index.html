Indonesian Family Offices and Entrepreneurs Investing in Switzerland

Indonesia's growing class of UHNWI and family business owners is beginning to look at Switzerland for capital preservation and European exposure. GKP has on-the-ground experience in both markets.

This article is for informational purposes only and does not constitute tax, legal, or investment advice. Readers should consult a qualified Swiss tax advisor, lawyer, or financial professional before making any decisions.

Indonesia's economic growth has created a substantial class of first and second generation UHNWI, entrepreneurs, conglomerates, and family businesses spanning resources, property, technology, and finance. As these families mature, international portfolio diversification becomes a priority. Switzerland is consistently among their preferred destinations in Europe.

What Switzerland Offers Indonesian Investors

Swiss real estate in Zurich, Geneva, and Zug offers long-term value preservation in one of the world's most stable legal and political environments. For Indonesian family offices managing multigenerational capital, Swiss assets serve as a reliable anchor outside the region. The Swiss franc is a natural hedge against emerging market currency risk.

Beyond real estate, Swiss SME acquisitions offer Indonesian entrepreneurs an operational foothold in Europe, with access to EU markets, Swiss engineering talent, and a globally respected business address. GKP sources these opportunities off-market through established Swiss relationships.

Lex Koller and Indonesian Buyers

Indonesian nationals without Swiss or EU/EFTA residency are subject to Lex Koller restrictions on residential real estate. Commercial property and business acquisitions are generally not restricted. Structuring via Swiss entities is a common and effective approach for residential mandates.
